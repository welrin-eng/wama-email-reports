What We're Testing in June
Test 01
Higher CTA placement
Moving the primary CTA above the fold in 3 June campaigns to test whether earlier visibility lifts click rate on emails that are already opening at 60%+.
Hypothesis: If subscribers are interested enough to open, removing scroll friction should increase clicks on high-open campaigns.
Test 02
Welcome Series subject line A/B test
Testing two different subject line styles across the Welcome Series to find what drives stronger open rates. Variant A uses a curiosity-driven question. Variant B leads with a direct product benefit. Running across new subscribers entering the flow in June.
Hypothesis: The Welcome Series open rate is solid at 38.3% but has room to grow. Finding a stronger subject line formula here compounds across every new subscriber who enters the flow.
Test 03
First Purchase Anniversary - content and design refresh
The Anniversary flow opens at 79.3% but has zero clicks. Testing a redesigned email with a prominent CTA button, a clearer offer, and simplified layout to convert that engagement into purchases. Running as an A/B test against the existing version once the CTA link issue is confirmed fixed.
Hypothesis: A 79% open rate with 0% clicks is a layout or link problem, not an audience problem. Fixing the CTA and simplifying the design should unlock purchases from a highly engaged segment.
What to Watch in June
Click rates - testing stronger CTAs and simpler layouts
Opens are consistently above 60%, but most campaigns aren't converting that to clicks. In June we'll test higher CTA placement, fewer distractions in the email body, and stronger call-to-action copy to close this gap.
Wallet Balance segment - grow the audience
The Wallet Balance Reminder earned $0.34 per recipient - the highest RPR of any campaign. The list is only 898 people. Growing this segment and building a dedicated rewards flow is a low-effort, high-return opportunity.
Re-engagement flow - refresh underway
The Re-engagement 30D flow is underperforming against benchmarks. Updating subject lines and copy in June to give lapsed subscribers a better reason to come back, with a soft offer to drive action.

Steal From Competitors
Steal 01NADS
Named bundle tiers - "Get a Bundle + Save Up to 40%"
NADS introduced three named bundle tiers in May: Tactical Set (2 pairs, 25% off), Heavy Hitter (3 pairs, 35% off), Kings Pack (4+ pairs, 40% off). Preview line: "Use that cash for beer." Volume-based savings, not urgency-based - disciplined offer management that lifts AOV without training the list to wait for flash sales. It was the only time they exceeded 15% off all month.
WAMA Version
A Boxer Briefs + Trunks bundle campaign with named tiers. "The Starter" (2 pairs, 10% off) - "The Full Week" (4 pairs, 15% off) - "The Upgrade" (6 pairs, 20% off). Send to Engaged 90D buyers who haven't purchased in 60+ days. AOV play - we've never run a structured bundle campaign. Boxer Briefs + Trunks is the natural starting pair.
Steal 02NADS
Quote-led subject line - "I Should Have Switched Years Ago"
NADS ran a customer testimonial email on May 16 using a direct customer quote as the subject line. No discount. No offer. The subject line did all the work - it reads as real, not polished. Quote-led subject lines consistently outperform brand-written ones because they feel like something a friend would say. NADS also used "upgraded" as a key word across several emails - framing the switch as an upgrade, not just a product change.
WAMA Version
Plain text email to Buyers + Engaged 180D. Pull a real customer review or Yotpo quote that echoes the "I should have switched" sentiment about hemp. Let the customer voice carry the email. No offer needed - social proof does the conversion work. Subject: pull the quote verbatim and let it stand alone.
Steal 03NADS
Gift angle targeting women buyers - "slip them in his drawer and wait"
NADS ran a gifting-angle email on May 7 targeting women buying underwear for their partner or boyfriend. No discount. The copy framed the purchase as a quiet upgrade the guy wouldn't buy himself - "slip them in his drawer and wait." No offer, no urgency - just a new buyer segment most underwear brands ignore. It opens a gifting funnel without any promotional pressure.
WAMA Version
A gift-framing campaign targeting female buyers or subscribers who haven't purchased (potential gift buyers). Tone: warm, slightly playful. "He won't buy hemp underwear himself. That's your move." No discount needed. Send around a natural gifting moment - Father's Day, birthday, or just a regular week as a non-seasonal angle. Soft CTA to shop as a gift.
